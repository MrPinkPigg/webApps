Barry B Benson, a young revolutionary, has just graduated college and is expected to join the rest of the factory workers making honey for the bourgeois. Barry enters the workforce along with his friend Joseph becoming one of the pollen-collectors, the workers who have to go outside the hive to gather materials. When the collectors take Barry to a flower patch for his first time, they end up getting separated leading him to be lost in New York City. Barry eventually finds his way to the balcony of a human florist named Vanessa. When her upper class boyfriend Ken see's him, he tries to step on him, But Vanessa stops him and lets Barry go free, thus saving his life. After his encounter with the bourgeois and Vanessa, Barry becomes conflicted over what his true purpose is. This is even more apparent when he notices that the upper class is taking advantage of all of the hard work from the lower class factory workers; selling their honey for their own profit while bees are struggling to survive Barry later returns to Vanessa, breaking one of the societal rules; Bees can't interact the upper class humans.
